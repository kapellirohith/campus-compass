Chapter 01

Before You Pack.

Don't bring your entire room. Bring exactly what you need. Most freshers overpack clothes and underpack crucial electronics and documents.

Documents (Crucial)

  • Aadhar Card (Original + 5 Copies)
  • 10th & 12th Marksheets (Originals)
  • Passport size photos (At least 15)
  • Seat Allotment Order (Printed)
  • Transfer & Migration Certificates

The Dress Code

  • Formals for class (Strictly enforced)
  • Casual wear for evenings/weekends
  • Sports gear & sneakers
  • Rain jacket/Umbrella (Heavy monsoons)
  • Light warm layer (AC classrooms are freezing)

Electronics & Hostel

  • Laptop + Charger (Non-negotiable)
  • Extension board with surge protector
  • Power bank (Crucial)
  • Bedsheet, pillow & thin blanket
  • Bucket & toiletries (Buy locally if needed)

Chapter 02

Your First 30 Days.

The first week will feel strange. You will walk in circles. Give it exactly 30 days to find your rhythm. Here is your roadmap.

Week 1 — Survive

Mood: Everything feels unfamiliar.
  • Learn your timetable
  • Find the mess without Google Maps
  • Get GPMS sorted
  • Learn where your classrooms are
  • Do not stay in your room all day

Week 2 — Settle

Mood: Things stop feeling scary.
  • Start talking to classmates
  • Join WhatsApp groups
  • Explore the library
  • Figure out laundry
  • Learn AUMS

Week 3 — Explore

Mood: The campus starts feeling smaller.
  • Attend club inductions
  • Visit facilities
  • Meet seniors
  • Attend at least one event

Week 4 — Build

Mood: You are no longer a visitor. You belong here.
  • Pick one technical goal
  • Pick one social goal
  • Pick one club

Insider Realities

The Food Situation

The mess is strictly vegetarian. The first week, it tastes very different from home cooking. Do not survive entirely on Maggi from the canteen; your stomach will give up by mid-terms. Force yourself to eat the mess food until your palate adjusts.

The Laundry Truth

Doing your own laundry takes hours you don't have. Figure out the campus laundry service schedule immediately and budget for it. It is the best investment of your allowance.

The Local Town

Mangalagiri is a developing area. For major shopping or movies, you will need to head to Vijayawada or Guntur. Plan these trips for weekends, and always ensure your GPMS out-pass is approved a day in advance.

Chapter 03

Inside The Classroom.

Every semester, someone discovers the FA grade the hard way.

If your attendance drops below 75%, you receive an FA (Failure by Attendance). This means you do not even get to sit for the end-semester exam. It is not "low marks." You literally cannot enter the hall. Attendance is marked every single hour. Check AUMS every Friday. Do not volunteer for this lesson.

The CGPA Survival Strategy

  • Semester 4 is the deadline.

    Your CGPA is basically cemented by the end of your second year. After that, each new grade barely moves the needle. A 7.5 in first year is nearly impossible to pull up to 9.0 later. Start incredibly strong.

  • Understand Relative Grading.

    Your 72% might be an 'A' or a 'B+' — it entirely depends on what the rest of the class scored. When everyone studies hard, the bar rises for everyone. You are competing against the curve.

  • Lab courses carry serious weight.

    A lazy lab report can drag your SGPA down more than a bad quiz. Write them properly, submit them on time, and actually understand the code you wrote in the lab.

  • Your own class notes will save you.

    Professors literally say "this is important for the exam" during lectures. If you're not there, you miss the hint. If you're there but not writing it down, you still miss it.

  • 8.5 is the magic number.

    Below an 8.5 CGPA, you won't even see some top company shortlists during placement season. Above that, doors open automatically. Knowing the rules early means you can play to win.

The Amrita Dictionary

You will hear these acronyms on Day 1 and panic. Here is exactly what they mean.

AUMS
Amrita University Management System. Your grades, attendance, and fee receipts live here. Bookmark it.
FA
Failure by Attendance. Below 75% attendance gets you this grade, preventing you from writing the exam.
CSAP
Centralised Seat Allotment Process. The online system that brought you here.
GPMS
Gate Pass Management System. You literally cannot leave the campus gates without this digitally approved.
SGPA
Semester Grade Point Average. Your score for one specific semester. It resets every six months.
CGPA
Cumulative Grade Point Average. Your overall score across all completed semesters. This is what companies look at.
OD
On Duty. When you skip class for an official college event or competition. Get this approved BEFORE the event.
DC
Disciplinary Committee. You do not want to meet them. Follow the rules and stay off their radar.
AEEE
Amrita Entrance Exam — Engineering. The test that started this whole journey.
Proxy
Marking attendance for someone who isn't actually in the classroom. They check. It is not worth the DC action.
Chapter 04

Things We Learned The Hard Way.

"I thought first year CGPA didn't matter."

I came to college thinking placements were years away.

Semester 1 felt harmless.

I skipped assignments. Studied only before exams. Told myself I would become serious later.

Later arrived much faster than expected.

When opportunities started appearing, my CGPA was already carrying the weight of those early decisions.

What I learned:

A strong start is easier than a comeback.

"I'll join clubs after I settle in."

Everyone says this.

Including me.

The plan sounded logical. First settle. Then make friends. Then join clubs.

A month passed. Then a semester.

Then suddenly the people who joined during week one were leading events while I was still thinking about applying.

What I learned:

You do not need confidence before joining. You join first. Confidence comes later.

"I'll watch the lecture recording later."

No. You won't.

I won't. Almost nobody does.

One missed class becomes two. Two become five.

Then you spend the night before an exam trying to learn an entire unit in panic mode.

What I learned:

Future me was never more disciplined than present me.

"Everybody else seems smarter than me."

During the first month it felt like everyone already knew coding.

Everyone already had friends. Everyone already knew how college worked.

The truth?

Most people were confused. Some were simply better at hiding it.

What I learned:

Never compare your first chapter to someone else's chapter twenty.

"My first friend group will be my forever friend group."

College doesn't work like that.

Some people become strangers. Unexpected people become family.

The people you sit with during orientation may not be the people sitting beside you during graduation.

What I learned:

Let friendships grow naturally. Don't force permanence.

"I'll start coding after I understand everything."

This is probably the most expensive lie I told myself.

I kept waiting. For the perfect roadmap. The perfect course. The perfect YouTube playlist. The perfect moment.

None of them arrived.

Progress started only when I stopped preparing and started building.

What I learned:

Start badly. Improve publicly.

If you forget everything else in this handbook, remember this:

Nobody arrives at Amrita fully prepared. Nobody has everything figured out.

The students who grow aren't the ones who avoid mistakes. They're the ones who recover from them quickly.

You'll make some mistakes too. Just try not to make the expensive ones.

"I lost 0.4 CGPA in semester 1 because I thought first year didn't matter. It took me 3 semesters to recover. Don't be me."

— Senior Confession
Chapter 05

Beyond The Classroom.

How Students Actually Get Into Clubs

Nobody joins because they are already skilled. Most students join because they are curious.

Watch for induction announcements during your first month. Fill the forms. Show up. Talk to people. Attempt the tasks even if you feel underqualified. The students leading these communities were once freshers who thought they weren't ready.

The Biggest Club Mistake

Every year, students tell themselves: "I'll join after I settle in." Then a month becomes a semester. The students who joined during week one become organizers. The students who waited remain spectators. Join early. You can always leave later. The opposite is much harder.

If You Join One Technical Community, Make It This One.

Chakravyuha Technical Club is the club behind this entire challenge.

If you want hackathons, projects, AI, technical events, workshops, and people who constantly build things outside class, start here. This is one of the fastest ways to stop feeling like a fresher and start becoming part of the technical community.

Chapter 06

The Unwritten Rules.

Nobody will sit you down and tell you these things. Read them carefully before Day 1.

Only At Amaravati

GPMS Realities: The servers slow down on Friday afternoon because everyone is applying for weekend passes. Apply on Thursday if you want to leave campus smoothly.

Weekend Travel: Mangalagiri is quiet. If you want to watch a movie or go to a major mall, you will need to plan a trip to Vijayawada or Guntur. Form groups early to split auto fares.

The Library: It is the only place on campus where you can truly escape the hostel chaos. Use it heavily during exam season. It fills up fast.

The Green List (Do This)

  • Start solving Data Structures & Algorithms from semester 1. Not the night before placements.
  • Walk into one club meeting where you know nobody.
  • Make friends with the seniors. They have the notes and the past year papers.
  • Use the Central Library. It's quiet, cool, and forces you to focus.
  • Back up your laptop regularly. Hardware fails right before mid-terms.

The Red List (Avoid This)

  • Don't chase only CGPA and ignore building actual, deployable projects.
  • Never skip the first week of classes. That's when project groups are permanently formed.
  • Don't try to proxy attendance. They check. It's not worth the Disciplinary action.
  • Don't stay in your room all weekend. Amaravati has places to explore if you look.
  • Don't assume 12th-grade cramming habits will work here. They absolutely won't.

If You Remember Only Five Things

  • 1. Attendance below 75% is dangerous. (FA grade is real).
  • 2. Join clubs early. (Month 1 is induction month).
  • 3. Build projects from semester 1. (CGPA alone isn't enough).
  • 4. Protect your CGPA early. (Semester 1 is the easiest time to score high).
  • 5. Make friends outside your branch. (Your network is your net worth).

Chapter 08

Questions Every Fresher Eventually Asks.

1. Is attendance really that strict?

Yes. 75% is a hard line. Fall below it and you get an FA (Failure by Attendance), meaning you cannot write the final exam. There are no exceptions.

2. I have never coded before. Am I already behind?

No. The introductory courses assume you know nothing. You have exactly one semester to catch up before the curve moves. Start building immediately.

3. Is hostel food actually bad?

It's not bad; it's just repetitive, and it's strictly vegetarian. The shock usually comes from the sudden change in spice levels and missing home cooking.

4. Does first-year CGPA really matter?

It matters more than any other year. It acts as the anchor for your entire degree. A high first-year CGPA gives you a massive buffer later on when subjects get harder.

5. Should I buy a laptop before joining?

Yes, it is practically mandatory. You cannot survive an engineering degree doing assignments on your phone or borrowing laptops. Invest in a solid machine.

6. When should I join clubs?

In your first two months. Watch out for induction announcements. Don't wait until you feel "ready" or "skilled enough" — nobody is ready. Just show up.

7. What if I feel homesick?

Everyone does, even if they pretend otherwise. Call home often, but force yourself to sit in the common areas or the library instead of isolating in your room.

8. Is everyone else more prepared than me?

No. Most people are completely lost and just very good at acting confident. Don't let imposter syndrome ruin your first semester.
